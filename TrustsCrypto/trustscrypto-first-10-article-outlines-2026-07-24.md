## Shared brief rules (mandatory for all articles)

- H2 skeleton is mandatory in order: What Did / Why Matters / Who Is Affected / What Remains Unclear / What to Watch
- "What Remains Unclear / What Has and Has Not Been Verified" H2 is NON-NEGOTIABLE in every article
- Word count: 650-1,000. No padding. Scope narrowly rather than pad.
- Schema: NewsArticle only. No FAQ schema. No HowTo.
- Lists: <ul> only -- for on-chain data points (addresses, tx hashes, numerical evidence). No <ol>. No <table>.
- Source language preserved: if source used "preliminary," quote and flag it
- Pending confirmation flag required in every article where a key claim remains unverified
- Banned: surged, exploded, massive, game-changer, bullish signal, moon, significant (alone), thousands of (unless sourced)

## Article 1 -- MiCA Stablecoin Regulation Explained

**Primary keyword:** `mica stablecoin regulation explained`
**H1:** What MiCA's Stablecoin Rules Actually Require: Issuer Obligations and Timeline
**Pillar:** /news/
**Path:** `/news/mica-stablecoin-regulation-explained`
**Word count target:** 700-900

**Mandatory H2 skeleton:**
1. What MiCA's Stablecoin Rules Actually Require
2. Why These Requirements Matter for Issuers and Users in the EU
3. Which Stablecoin Issuers and Products Are in Scope
4. What Remains Unclear or Subject to EBA Technical Standards
5. What Issuers and Users Should Watch Before the Compliance Deadline

**Lead:** "The Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) imposed licensing requirements on stablecoin issuers operating in the European Union from June 2024, per the official EUR-Lex text (Regulation EU 2023/1114). Issuers of e-money tokens (EMTs) and asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) must hold authorization from a national competent authority or cease EU operations."

**Primary sources to cite:**
- EUR-Lex full text: Regulation EU 2023/1114
- EBA stablecoin technical standards (RTS/ITS published dates)
- Official ESMA guidance where available
- Any licensed issuer announcements (Circle, Société Générale Forge EURCV, etc.)

**Key facts to include:**
- EMT definition and ART definition (from the regulation, not from interpretation)
- Reserve requirement: 1:1 backing, held in segregated accounts, minimum 30% in credit institution deposits
- Significant EMT threshold: >€5B average outstanding or >10M transactions/day triggers enhanced supervision
- USDT status: Tether not licensed under MiCA as of Q3 2024 -- Binance delisted USDT for EU users
- Deadline: June 30, 2024 (initial), with transition periods for pre-existing tokens

**What remains unclear section must include:**
- EBA's position on algorithmic stablecoins under the ART definition
- How third-country issuers serving EU users are treated without an EU entity
- Technical standards for reserve audit frequency (still being finalized as of publication date)

**Signature moves:**
- Source language preservation: MiCA uses "authorization" not "approval" -- use the regulation's own term
- Scale separation: "licensed to operate" is different from "compliant with all technical standards" -- distinguish them

- "Activated" is the correct term for Ethereum upgrades, not "launched" or "released"
- Distinguish EIPs by layer: consensus layer (beacon chain) vs. execution layer (EVM)

## Article 10 -- Bitcoin Mining Difficulty Explained

**Primary keyword:** `bitcoin mining difficulty explained`
**H1:** What Bitcoin's Mining Difficulty Adjustment Actually Does and What the Latest Data Shows
**Pillar:** /news/bitcoin/
**Path:** `/news/bitcoin/bitcoin-mining-difficulty-explained`
**Word count target:** 700-900

**Mandatory H2 skeleton:**
1. What the Mining Difficulty Adjustment Actually Does and Why It Exists
2. Why the Adjustment Matters for Miners, Block Times, and Network Security
3. Which Miners and Mining Operations Are Most Affected by the Current Level
4. What the Latest Difficulty Data Shows and What Remains Variable
5. What Miners and the Network Should Watch at the Next Adjustment Epoch

**Lead:** "Bitcoin's mining difficulty adjusted [up/down] by [X]% at block [number] on [date], per data from mempool.space and Glassnode. The adjustment brought the difficulty to [Y], the [highest/lowest] level since [date]."

**Primary sources to cite:**
- mempool.space difficulty data (linked)
- Glassnode difficulty chart (linked or cited with date)
- Luxor or Hashrate Index for hashrate data (linked)

**Key facts to include:**
- Current difficulty level (exact, in scientific notation if necessary)
- Percentage change from prior epoch
- Current estimated hashrate and what it implies for difficulty at next adjustment
- Block time over the prior 2016 blocks: above or below 10 minutes? (cite mempool.space)
- Next adjustment: estimated date and estimated direction (up/down)
- Miner revenue context: current fee per block vs. subsidy per block

**What remains unclear section must include:**
- Whether large mining operations have announced hashrate changes that would materially affect next adjustment
- How network security changes if hashrate concentration increases (cite mining pool shares if recent)
- Relationship between difficulty and miner profitability at current BTC price (present as a question, not a claim)

**Signature moves:**
- Difficulty adjustment is algorithmic and automatic -- do not describe it as a decision made by anyone
- "Estimated hashrate" is inferred from blocks found -- it is not directly observable; state this
- Distinguish "hashrate increased" from "difficulty increased" -- they are different metrics with a ~2-week lag
