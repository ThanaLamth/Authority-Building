## The top Bitcoin ETFs by AUM in 2026 are still led by IBIT, FBTC, GBTC, and a smaller second tier including ARKB and BITB

The Bitcoin ETFs that still dominate the category in 2026 are led by BlackRock’s iShares Bitcoin Trust, Fidelity Wise Origin Bitcoin Fund, and Grayscale Bitcoin Trust ETF, with a second tier that typically includes ARK 21Shares Bitcoin ETF and Bitwise Bitcoin ETF. After that, the comparison usually broadens into VanEck HODL, WisdomTree BTCW, Invesco BTCO, Franklin EZBC, and Valkyrie BRRR.

That leadership group matters because ETF competition is now largely about distribution strength, brand trust, fees, and how sticky the flows remain after the initial launch cycle.

## The full list

### 1. BlackRock iShares Bitcoin Trust

IBIT remains the clearest AUM leader to compare because BlackRock’s distribution power changed the entire market structure around US spot Bitcoin ETFs. Even readers who dislike broad financialization need to understand that IBIT became a benchmark for institutional demand.

Its strength is scale and reach. Its risk is that many readers confuse the success of one ETF wrapper with a verdict on Bitcoin’s original self-custody thesis.

### 2. Fidelity Wise Origin Bitcoin Fund

FBTC stays near the top because Fidelity remains one of the strongest traditional-finance brands bridging retail and institutional asset allocation. It is one of the first funds serious comparison readers check after IBIT.

Its main strength is credibility. Its main limitation is that it competes in a category where distribution is so concentrated that even a strong second-place position still exists in BlackRock’s shadow.

### 3. Grayscale Bitcoin Trust ETF

GBTC remains structurally important because it carries legacy scale and history. Even after the market evolved beyond the old trust structure, Grayscale stayed central to how many investors think about listed Bitcoin exposure.

The risk is that history cuts both ways. GBTC matters because it is large, but it also remains tied to a more complicated legacy narrative than some newer entrants.

Readers who need the structural distinction rather than the ranking should move next to the existing Coincu explainer on [Bitcoin spot ETF versus Bitcoin futures ETF in 2026](https://coincu.com/bitcoin-spot-etf-vs-futures-etf/).

### 4. ARK 21Shares Bitcoin ETF

ARKB matters because it has stayed part of the smaller but still meaningful group behind the three largest names. It is useful as a comparison point for readers trying to understand where the long tail of serious ETF assets begins.

### 5. Bitwise Bitcoin ETF

BITB belongs here because Bitwise remains one of the more visible crypto-specialist brands in the ETF category. That gives it relevance beyond raw size.

### 6. VanEck Bitcoin ETF

VanEck matters because it is one of the recognizable ETF-native issuers competing in the space, and it tends to remain in serious comparison sets even when not leading the board.

### 7. WisdomTree Bitcoin Fund

BTCW belongs in the conversation because it represents another established asset-management brand trying to compete in the Bitcoin wrapper market.

### 8. Invesco Galaxy Bitcoin ETF

BTCO matters because it joins a traditional ETF distribution brand with a crypto-native research and market identity.

### 9. Franklin Bitcoin ETF

EZBC matters because Franklin Templeton has shown broader commitment to digital-asset product development beyond a single ETF line.

### 10. Valkyrie Bitcoin Fund

BRRR rounds out the top comparison set because it remains one of the better-known names from the first wave of post-approval spot Bitcoin ETF competition.

## FAQ

### What does AUM actually tell you in a Bitcoin ETF ranking?

AUM tells you where capital has concentrated. It is useful for measuring category leadership, but it does not automatically tell you which fund is cheapest or best for every investor.

### Why do IBIT, FBTC, and GBTC matter more than the rest?

Because they sit at the center of distribution, flows, and category perception. Even when smaller funds remain viable, most market attention clusters around the leaders.

### Should investors use ETF AUM as the only decision factor?

No. It should be one factor alongside fees, trading liquidity, tax context, platform availability, and whether the investor actually wants listed exposure instead of direct Bitcoin ownership.
