# MarketBit Content Production Plan

Date: 2026-07-09

This plan converts the MarketBit topic research and article briefs into a structured production batch.

## Goal

Produce 10 high-quality draft articles that are materially better than short rewrite-style crypto news posts and can become the foundation of stronger topic clusters.

## Output Structure

- `docs/marketbit-topic-research-2026-07-08.md`
- `docs/marketbit-first-10-article-briefs-2026-07-09.md`
- `docs/articles/01-...md` through `docs/articles/10-...md`
- `docs/marketbit-content-tracker-2026-07-09.md`

## Editorial Strategy

### What MarketBit should do

- Publish fewer pieces with stronger context.
- Build repeatable topic systems instead of chasing every headline.
- Prioritize evergreen explainers, analysis, and comparison pages.
- Use timely hooks only when they support a durable article angle.

### What MarketBit should avoid

- thin single-source rewrites
- generic price-analysis filler
- press-release style posts mixed into core editorial coverage
- unsupported legal or market claims
- fake authority language that the site cannot support

## Draft Standard

Each article draft must include:

- a clear H1
- a strong intro that answers the query early
- H2/H3 structure that maps to search intent
- one snippet-ready definition or summary block
- a realistic conclusion
- an editor source checklist
- internal-link suggestions
- `[needs source]` markers where publication-grade verification is still required

## Acceptance Criteria

A draft is considered complete for this batch when:

1. The angle is useful without depending on one news event.
2. The piece is structurally ready for editing and publishing.
3. The primary keyword appears naturally in the title, intro, and at least one subheading.
4. Any sensitive factual claim that still needs validation is marked `[needs source]`.
5. The article can connect into at least one larger cluster.

## Source Workflow

### Allowed source posture for this batch

- use top-tier outlet reporting for news hooks and recent developments
- use direct company, issuer, or regulatory documents for final publication checks
- treat these drafts as source-aware but not fully source-locked

### Before publish, verify

- dates
- company names
- asset amounts
- license scope
- legal terminology
- stablecoin reserve or market-share numbers
- product structure claims
