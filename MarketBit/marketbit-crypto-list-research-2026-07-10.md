# MarketBit Crypto List Research

Date: 2026-07-10

This document captures the research logic behind the 10 crypto list articles added in the July 10 batch.

## Goal

Build authority-style list content that fits a crypto narrative newsroom rather than a generic affiliate site.

## Chosen topic set

1. Crypto regulators to watch in 2026
2. Biggest crypto lawsuits of 2026
3. Biggest crypto fraud cases in 2026
4. Top crypto influencers in 2026
5. Top crypto VC firms in 2026
6. Largest crypto exchanges in 2026
7. Biggest crypto exchange collapses
8. Biggest crypto hacks of 2026 so far
9. Biggest crypto crashes in history, updated for 2026
10. Most influential people in crypto in 2026

## Why this batch works

- It turns a narrative-journalism angle into searchable list formats.
- It mixes people, institutions, conflicts, investigations, and market structure.
- It creates reusable annual franchises rather than one-off posts.

## Search-intent pattern

Most of the batch is informational, but not beginner-only informational.

The user intent usually falls into one of these buckets:

- who matters now
- what failed and why
- which institutions control outcomes
- what the market should learn from a ranking or collapse

## Structural rule used in the drafts

Every article follows the same logic:

1. the first H2 answers the title directly
2. a methodology section explains the ranking logic
3. the list itself carries the body of the article
4. a short “what this tells us” section turns the list into analysis
5. the page closes with FAQ, source list, and internal link targets

## Notes on evidence quality

- The regulators, VC, hacks, and exchange pieces have the strongest current-source footing.
- The historical collapse article is solid as a draft but should get more case-specific links before final publication.
- The lawsuits and fraud pieces are intentionally written as legal-impact explainers rather than narrow docket summaries.
