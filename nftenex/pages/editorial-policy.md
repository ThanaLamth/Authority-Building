# NFTEnex Editorial Policy

NFTEnex publishes editorial comparisons, guides, reviews, and explainers focused on NFTs, digital ownership, creator tools, marketplaces, infrastructure, and culture. Our goal is to help readers understand what is changing, what matters, and where the real tradeoffs are.

We do not treat every article as the same kind of evidence. Some pages are documentation-backed editorial comparisons. Some include original screenshots from public product surfaces. Some may later include deeper hands-on testing. The important thing is that we do not blur the line between those evidence levels.

## What We Cover

NFTEnex covers topics such as:

- NFT minting tools
- NFT marketplaces
- creator royalties
- NFT APIs and infrastructure
- metadata and storage
- NFT communities and culture
- gaming NFTs and digital assets

We aim to explain not only what a product or ecosystem claims to do, but also how it presents itself, who it appears to be built for, and where a reader should be cautious.

## How We Review Content

Our editorial process starts with the most direct evidence available at the time of review.

That may include:

- live public product surfaces
- official documentation
- help-center articles
- pricing or policy pages
- visible workflow cues
- original screenshots captured during review

When budget, access, or platform restrictions make full end-to-end testing unrealistic, we publish transparent editorial comparisons rather than pretending to have completed funded live workflows we did not run.

## Evidence Standards

We distinguish between several evidence levels.

### 1. Public-surface review

This means we directly reviewed:

- homepage messaging
- public product navigation
- public feature pages
- public docs or help articles
- visible onboarding cues

This is useful for understanding product posture, clarity, audience fit, and visible workflow structure.

### 2. Documentation-backed evaluation

This means we relied on official docs, help pages, pricing pages, or policy pages to verify claims that are not fully visible on the homepage alone.

This is useful for:

- fee logic
- royalty language
- chain support
- API positioning
- storage behavior
- platform rules

### 3. Hands-on workflow testing

This means we completed some part of a real setup, dashboard, API, wallet, checkout, or onchain flow ourselves and documented the result with original evidence.

If an article includes this level of evidence, we aim to say so clearly instead of implying it vaguely.

## What We Do Not Do

We do not intentionally:

- claim direct use where none happened
- present vendor marketing language as independent proof
- describe a funded onchain result as first-hand if we did not run it
- hide limitations that affect the strength of a conclusion

If we only reviewed public surfaces and documentation, we say that clearly.

## Original Visual Evidence

Where possible, NFTEnex includes original screenshots captured during review.

We use these screenshots to show:

- how a product presents its workflow
- how a marketplace frames discovery or trading
- how documentation explains a fee, storage, or API concept
- what stood out in the public experience

We do not treat stock images or third-party screenshots as first-hand evidence.

## How We Handle No-Budget Reviews

Some comparisons are published under a no-budget editorial model. In those cases, we prioritize:

- current public product surfaces
- official references
- visible workflow cues
- clear tradeoff analysis
- transparent limitations

In these articles, we do not overstate direct experience. If a conclusion would require:

- a funded wallet
- a paid account tier
- a live transaction
- a private dashboard workflow
- a completed secondary sale

we treat that as a limitation unless the workflow was actually completed and documented.

## Ranking and Recommendations

When we publish a "best" or "top" article, the ranking reflects editorial judgment, not a universal truth.

We usually weigh:

- workflow clarity
- user fit
- product posture
- visible tradeoffs
- documentation quality
- ecosystem relevance
- practical usefulness for the type of reader the article targets

That means the best choice for one reader may not be the best choice for another. We try to make those differences explicit.

## Updates and Refreshes

NFT markets and product surfaces change quickly. We refresh articles when important claims may have shifted.

We pay special attention to:

- fees
- chain support
- live availability
- policy updates
- API or marketplace positioning
- screenshot accuracy

Each reviewed article should include a visible last-reviewed date so readers can judge freshness.

## Corrections Policy

If we discover that a factual claim is outdated, unclear, or wrong, we update it as quickly as possible.

This may include:

- correcting a sentence
- replacing a screenshot
- changing a recommendation
- narrowing a claim
- adding a clearer limitation note

If a mistake materially changes the meaning of the page, we prefer correcting the article itself rather than silently leaving weak wording in place.

## Commercial Independence

NFTEnex aims to keep editorial judgment separate from promotional pressure.

If a page is:

- sponsored
- promotional
- a press release
- an advertising placement

it should be labeled clearly and should not be presented as an ordinary independent comparison article.

## Reader Guidance

Readers should treat NFTEnex content as editorial research and decision support, not as legal, tax, or financial advice.

NFT tools, marketplaces, and asset ecosystems can change rapidly. Before committing money, assets, or private keys, readers should verify current conditions on the official product or platform they plan to use.

## Contact and Feedback

If you believe an NFTEnex article contains an error or outdated claim, contact the editorial team with:

- the article URL
- the sentence or claim in question
- the official source that supports the correction

Clear correction requests help us update faster and keep the site more trustworthy.
