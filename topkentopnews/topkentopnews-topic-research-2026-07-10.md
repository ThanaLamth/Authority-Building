# topkentopnews Topic Research

Date: 2026-07-10

## Scope

The site shape supplied for this batch centers on four editorial lanes:

- `insights`
- `trends`
- `narratives`
- `macro`

The goal of this batch is not to publish generic crypto listicles. It is to create search-facing authority pages that still fit a narrative and analysis brand.

## Working Assumptions

- Target market: English, global crypto readers
- Query type: top/list/watchlist intent with strong 2026 freshness signals
- Editorial standard: risk-first, explanatory, and non-promotional
- Monetization bias to avoid: affiliate-style "buy now" framing

## Why These Topics Won

### 1. They fit the taxonomy cleanly

Every keyword lands naturally inside an approved level-2 category instead of forcing a new structure.

### 2. They balance traffic and authority

The set mixes broader high-demand pages such as AI coins, memecoins, and altcoins with deeper authority pieces such as Bitcoin cycle indicators, institutional trends, and regulation.

### 3. They support internal-link loops

The package lets the site link:

- `trends` pages into `narratives`
- `narratives` pages into `insights`
- `insights` pages into `macro`
- `macro` pages back into `trends` and `narratives`

That loop is important because crypto search journeys rarely end on one page type.

## Content Rules For This Batch

### Direct-answer rule

The first H2 must answer the title directly in one move. Readers should know the shortlist or the main conclusion before they scroll into methodology.

### Method rule

Every page needs a visible ranking method. The point is not fake precision. The point is showing the lens:

- usage
- liquidity
- category relevance
- catalysts
- governance
- risk

### Tone rule

Do not write as if the list is a guarantee list. These are watchlists and ranking frameworks, not promises of returns.
